ORIGIN OF LOGAN’S NAME

Continuing William Logans legacy

Logan Aventura chose the name Logan in honor of William Logan, a well-known Scottish officer of the Madras Civil Service under the British Government, appointed by the Collector of Malabar. He wrote what we still view as a source book on Malabar. The involvement of Logan in demarcating the French Loge lands at Calicut & the Attapadi silent valley suit and the recording of facts and evidence contributed to the preservation of the Attapadi forest, including the Silent Valley, which ecologists in Kerala now mention with pride. Logan’s superiors were not happy about this, for Logan was appointed district and sessions judge in Cudappah, later he resigned and returned to Scotland, where he would lead a life of obscurity. Through Logan Aventura, he is continuing his legacy of Malabar.
